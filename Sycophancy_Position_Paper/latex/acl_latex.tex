\begin{abstract}
Sycophantic behavior is an established problem in AI systems, with concerns growing from users and researchers alike about the harmful effects of overly people-pleasing behavior in these models. However, there is disagreement between many researchers regarding what exactly counts as ``AI sycophancy'', and empirical studies primarily focus on a small subset of sycophantic behaviors with easily-measurable impacts on response quality. Further, few studies have evaluated the downstream impacts of sycophantic AI systems, particularly in the medium-to-long-term. In this position paper, we propose a unified definition of AI sycophancy, which involves displacement of context-relevant norms associated with approval-seeking behavior. We provide a framework for isolating, evaluating, and documenting sycophantic behaviors which prioritizes harm reduction and allows for a greater understanding of broader impacts. We outline a roadmap for this impact-centered framework, identify critical gaps in current evaluation practices, and provide recommendations regarding best practices and areas of future research. We believe that this framework is a step towards developing consensus, studying more diverse behaviors, and designing more effective evaluations for AI sycophancy.

\end{abstract}
